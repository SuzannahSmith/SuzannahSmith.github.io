Education

After graduating from Leo Hayes High School, I went on to study Computer Science at the University of New Brunswick. I am currently in my fourth year, and have a cumulative GPA of 4.3 (out of 4.3).

My favorite courses so far have been Intro to Artificial Intelligence, Natural Language Processing, and Net-centic Computing. I also enjoy studying languages, like German, French, Greek, and Spanish.

The Beaverbrook room at the Harriet Irving Library. One of my favorite places to study on campus!

UNB: beautiful in every season.

Work Experience

July - August 2013: My first official job was working at a day camp for kids in Quebec. This was part of the YMCA's Summer Work Student Exchange six-week program. It was a great opportunity to explore Quebec, practice French, and earn a good paycheck.

June 2014 - April 2015: The summer after I graduated from high school I began working at a Tim Hortons in Fredericton. I continued working there throughout my first year of university, but then moved on to the Computer Science co-op program.

May - August 2015: My first Computer Science internship was at Professional Quality Assurance (PQA) in Fredericton. I worked there from May to August 2015. Some of my tasks included manually testing websties, testing the content of video-based courses, and writing detailed reviews and bug reports.

Sept - Dec 2015: After returning to school, I worked as a tutor for first year students. I tutored in Java, Intro to Computer Science, and Discrete Mathmatics courses.

Jan-April 2016: For my second work term, I moved to Charlottetown, PEI to work at SpryPoint. I really enjoyed learning web development there, and worked on the backend and frontend of several web apps. I also performed some database management tasks.

SpryPoint's beautiful office in downtown Charlottetown.

Performing a user study with the HTC Vive at the Hochschule Bonn-Rhein-Sieg in Germany.

May - August 2016: While doing a summer study term back in Fredericton, I was hired by Natalia Stakhanova to develop the curriculum for a web design class for kids. I designed the course outline and materials for ten two-hour classes.

Sept - Dec 2016: I completed my third work term at Eigen Innovations in Fredericton. I focused mainly on building new features for the front end of their AngularJS web app. Working with the other members of the frontend team, I researched best practices and planned the major refactoring of the app. I also set up automated testing using Browserstack, and wrote unit tests.

At the same time, I helped teach a weekly web design class to a small group of young girls, with the materials that I designed. It was fun to see girls getting excited about the things they could build with technology. At the end of the course, each student had completed their own simple HTML and CSS website.

Jan - April 2017: Back at school, for my 5th semester.

May - August 2017: I had the amazing opportunity to travel to Germany as part of the RISE Germany scholarship program. I performed research on virtual agents at the Hochschule Bonn-Rhein-Sieg in Sankt Augustin, Germany. Part of my responsibilities included building a virtual reality scene, configuring and recording eye-tracking data, and conducting a prelimenary user study.

Sept 2017 - Present: I am currently finishing up my 6th study semester at UNB.

Volunteer Experience
